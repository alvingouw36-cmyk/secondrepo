Specs & Mini Reviews

Logitech G502 HERO

Connection: Wired

Sensor: HERO optical sensor (100-25,600 DPI)

Buttons: 11 programmable

Weight: ~121 g

Extras: Adjustable weights, RGB lighting, mechanical switch buttons

A feature-packed wired gaming mouse with excellent build and low latency, though slightly heavy for fast-swipe gamers

Razer Basilisk V3 Pro

Connection: Wireless (2.4 GHz / Bluetooth / USB-C wired)

Sensor: Focus Pro 30K Optical (up to 30,000 DPI)

Buttons: 11 programmable

Weight: ~112 g

Extras: HyperScroll tilt wheel, Chroma RGB, up to 90 h battery life

A premium wireless all-rounder with rich features and strong performance, but heavier than ultra-light specialists

SteelSeries Aerox 5

Connection: Wireless or wired

Sensor: TrueMove Air Optical (up to 18,000 DPI)

Buttons: 9 programmable

Weight: ~66 g

Extras: Honeycomb shell, AquaBarrier IP54 protection, RGB lighting

A versatile multi-genre choice offering lots of programmable buttons in a lightweight shell, though battery life suffers in wireless mode

Mad Catz R.A.T. PRO X3

Connection: Wired

Sensor: PMW3389 Optical (up to 16,000 DPI)

Buttons: Modular customizable layout

Weight: Adjustable (varies by setup)

Extras: Swappable palm rests, 3000 Hz polling rate, magnesium chassis

A highly customizable enthusiast mouse with strong specs and adjustable layout, but some users report durability and layout quirks

WLmouse east X Max Magnesium

Connection: Wireless (2.4 GHz / USB-C wired)

Sensor: PAW3950 HS (up to 26,000 DPI)

Buttons: 5 main buttons

Weight: ~42 g

Extras: 8,000 Hz polling rate, magnesium alloy frame, under 1 ms latency

An ultra-light magnesium-body gaming mouse with superb latency and build quality, though its skeletal shell design may not suit every grip
